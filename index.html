At UNICEF USA, we recognize that local communities are innovation hubs shaping child-friendly policies and practices.

Through UNICEF USA’s municipal partnerships – such as the Child Friendly Cities Initiative -- local governments, nonprofits, other stakeholders and children are collaborating to build child-friendly communities, promoting youth leadership and child-centered local governance. Join us in highlighting innovative practices, celebrating successes, and advocating for child rights through this quarterly newsletter. Together, we can build communities where every child is safe, educated, healthy, and respected.

In advance of International Youth Day 2023, the City of Houston, in partnership with UNICEF USA, officially became the first UNICEF Child Friendly City in the United States! The Mayor’s Office of Education and Youth Engagement - alongside city leaders, government agencies, service providers, and youth and families - conducted a citywide situational analysis on the state of children’s rights and successfully implemented a local action plan to address mental health, emergency preparedness, and youth civic engagement. This momentous recognition reaffirms the City of Houston’s commitment to building better communities for children and youth.

Superstar siblings Jennifer and Michael are not only busy with school, friends, and everything else that involves being a young person but happen to be at the forefront of youth participation in local governance in their home City of Houston.

Holding formal leadership positions in both UNICEF USA's National Youth Council and Youth Rep programs AND Houston's CFCI Youth Council, Jennifer and Michael are committed to advancing child rights. Since 2017, World Children's Day has been UNICEF’s annual Global Day of Action for children, promoting the importance of children’s rights around the world. This is because November 20 marks the anniversary of the adoption of the Convention on the Rights of the Child, an important international agreement that aims to protect the rights of children worldwide. This year, celebrate by empowering a young person to take the lead on issues they care about by doing a Youth Takeover with a local government office, business or organization!

Check out this toolkit with steps on how you help facilitate a Youth Takeover in your community.
